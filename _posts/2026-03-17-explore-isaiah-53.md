*March 2026*

This post was written for a beginner's Bible Study on Isaiah 53.

---

7.40: **Dot.** Introduce the group purpose: the aim of the Bible Explorers Group is to explore Christianity together by reading and discussing the Bible. This is a space where you don't have to be a Christian to come and study Christianity—it's open to all.

7.41: **Both.**Introduce yourselves as group leaders. What is your name, what do you do during the week, what service do you go to at church?

7.43. **Ben.** Introductory question to get people thinking/talking: why do you think our society values suffering and sacrifice for the sake of others? Where do you see this value play out in our world today?

7.49. **Ben.** Introduce the passage for the night—Isaiah 53. While the book is given the name Isaiah (after a prophet named Isaiah who lived in Israel around 700–800 B.C.), we don't know exactly who wrote the words down—it was likely a scribe or a series of scribes. Isaiah chapter 53 is seen by Christians to be a lengthy description of what the Messiah, or the Christ, would be like. These words describe a man who lived, suffered, died, and then lived again, all for the benefit of God's people.

7.50. **Dot.** Read Isaiah 53 together. Read aloud from up front.

7.55. **Dot.** Spend 10 minutes discussing in small groups: what questions does the passage raise for you? Are there particular words, phrases or concepts that you struggle to understand?

8.05. **Both.** Spend 10 minutes sharing these questions across groups—and try to provide answers from the front where helpful.

8.15. **Dot.** Big group discussion. Think about Jesus' life, death and resurrection. Which verses in Isaiah 53 do you think align with what we know about Jesus? Conversely, are there verses here where it is more difficult to see the alignment with Jesus?

8.25. **Ben** Small group discussion. How do you feel about Isaiah 53:6: "We all, like sheep, have gone astray, each of us has turned to our own way; and the Lord has laid on him the iniquity of us all." What does show about the Bibles' view of humans like you and me, and what does it teach us about God's attitude towards people like you and me?

8.30. **Dot** Thank you for being here! Feel free to hang out and chat. Ben will need to lock up at (give ideal wrap-up time). See you next week!
