# Week 1: Belief in God

• Introduction / get-to-know-you question: **have you ever been on a journey?**

• There are two kinds of maps: a **large-scale map** that has lots of detail of a small area, and a **small-scale map** that has a small amount of detail but covers a large area. Both maps are highly valuable depending on your goal. Reading the Bible is like reading through a collection of large-scale maps—it takes a long time but you get more detail as you go.

• A summary of Christianity is like a small-scale map: it seeks to cover a large area but only with a small amount of detail.

• Course overview:
— Week 1. Belief in God
— Week 2. Father, Son, Spirit
— Week 3. Life Everlasting

#### An introduction to the Apostles' Creed

• One famous summary of Christian beliefs can be found in **the Apostles' Creed**.

• Who were the **Apostles**? An apostle is someone sent on a mission, often with a message to deliver. When Christians talk about "the Apostles", we're referring to those who Jesus sent out after his resurrection with the mission of spreading the message of his resurrection to the world.

• What is a **creed**? The word "creed" comes from the Latin "credo" which means "belief".

• What is the Apostles' Creed? It is a summary statement of what Jesus' Apostles believed and taught. It was not written by the Apostles; we don't know exactly who wrote it. All we know is that it was written by Christians in the first few centuries of the church to summarise the Christian faith—they used it to teach others what they believed and they recited it to remind one another about their core beliefs.

• It reads as follows:
*I believe in God, the Father almighty,
creator of heaven and earth.
I believe in Jesus Christ, his only Son, our Lord,
who was conceived by the Holy Spirit
and born of the virgin Mary.
He suffered under Pontius Pilate,
was crucified, died, and was buried;
he descended to hell.
The third day he rose again from the dead.
He ascended to heaven
and is seated at the right hand of God the Father almighty.
From there he will come to judge the living and the dead.
I believe in the Holy Spirit,
the holy catholic church,
the communion of saints,
the forgiveness of sins,
the resurrection of the body,
and the life everlasting. Amen.*

• In week 1 we will focus on exploring the words **"I believe in God"**.

#### "I"

• "I" is significant. Some people think that all people in the West are basically Christian. Some people think they are Christians because they went to a certain school or were baptised as babies or because their parents were Christians. But to be a Christian is to have a personal trust in God, not simply defined by culture or upbringing.

#### "believe in"

• "Believe in" need unpacking. It's not like saying "I believe in UFOs" – i.e. it is not simply saying that you believe God exists. It's not like saying "I believe in democracy" – i.e. that belief in God is a good idea that benefits society. It's more like saying "I believe in my car" right before you embark on a long trip. You entrust your livelihood to the reliability of the car. To believe in God is to entrust your life to Him.

• One verse that highlights what faith is about is found in Paul's letter to the church in Rome. It's describing a man who lived his life with faith, and it says he acted in faith **"being fully persuaded that God had power to do what he had promised." (Romans 4:21)**. Now—this is man whose faith was incredibly strong, and it's what we aim for as Christians, though there is such thing as "weak" faith as well. As Christians our faith is not always perfect, but we try to grow in our faith in God.

#### "God"

• The word "God" needs explaining. But first, it is worth acknowledging that for many people, even thinking about the idea of God is difficult because there are objections, or mental barriers, that need addressing.

• One objection to Christianity is: **doesn't science disproves God?** However, this is a misunderstanding of what science can and can't do. Science can give us a detailed description of the universe and how it works, but it cannot give us an explanation for why the universe exists to begin with. Science is about making observations about how the natural world behaves. It takes faith to say that the natural world always behaves consistently, and it takes faith to say that the natural world is all there is. You can believe in the usefulness of science and believe in God—in fact, most Christians do.

• Another objection to Christianity is: **isn't it arrogant to claim to know the one true God?** However, Christians only claim to know anything about God because we believe God has revealed himself to people. How? In history, through events in the Old Testament and in the events of the New Testament. We believe God has most clearly revealed himself through the person of Jesus—his life, death and resurrection.

• One verse that highlights this idea is: **"No one has ever seen God, but the one and only Son, who is himself God and is in closest relationship with the Father, has made him known." (John 1:18).**

Another objection to Christianity is: **Aren't all gods the same?** However, the Christian believes that God has revealed himself to humanity through the person of Jesus, and in particular his life, death and resurrection. Jews don't believe Jesus is God—they believe he died but didn't rise. Muslims don't believe Jesus is God—they believe he was a prophet, and didn't die on the cross. Buddhist belief doesn't really focus on God, and it doesn't really touch on Jesus. Hindu belief can include multiple gods, and does not touch on Jesus. If Jesus is God, and the path to knowing God is by understanding Jesus' death and resurrection, it is ignorant to say that all the major religions are the same.

• So, what kind of God do Christians believe in? One passage that explores God's character is: **"[God is a] compassionate and gracious God, slow to anger, abounding in love and faithfulness, maintaining love to thousands, and forgiving wickedness, rebellion and sin. Yet he does not leave the guilty unpunished." (Exodus 34:6-7)**.

• This is a spiritual claim about who God is. Some people become Christians because these spiritual claims resonate with them. Some people become Christians because they would much rather live in a universe where there is a God than one in which we are alone with no hope beyond death. Some people become Christians because they enjoy the community of Christians and so choose to commit to the Christian worldview. But for those who are sceptical, there is another reason to believe—Jesus was real, he really died, and there is good evidence to suggest he really rose. That is, the spiritual claims of Christianity are tenable because we can investigate the history on which they sit.

• Christianity involves belief in both the **spiritual and historical**.

• A verse which highlights this connection between the spiritual and the historical claims of Christianity: **"He was delivered over to death for our sins and was raised to life for our justification." (Romans 4:25)**,

• The historical claims are there to be investigated: **he was delivered over to death** and **he was raised to life**.

• The spiritual claims are there to be contemplated: he did this **for our sins** and **for our justification**.

# Week 2: Father, Son, Spirit

• Introductory / get-to-know-you question: **how would you describe yourself in 3 words?**

• Course overview / recap:
— Week 1. Belief in God
— Week 2. Father, Son, Spirit
— Week 3. Life Everlasting

• Read the Apostles' Creed

• **Trinity = 1 God, 3 persons**. Christians hold that there is one God—not multiple. Christians also hold that God exists as three persons—Father, Son, Spirit. This is mysterious, and nobody understands it perfectly. We believe that God is a tri-unity because this is how God reveals Himself to people.

• Christians believe in the trinity because the Bible speaks of the trinity. It served as a sort of greeting, or blessing, in the early church. Paul writes: **"May the grace of the Lord Jesus Christ, and the love of God, and the fellowship of the Holy Spirit be with you all." (2 Corinthians 13:14)**.

• To explore the persons of the trinity, we will unpack some lines from the Apostles' Creed that describe the Father and the Son—next week we will discuss the Spirit. We won't have time to cover everything but if there are lines you have questions about, ask those questions in question time.

#### Father

• ***God, the Father Almighty***. We believe God is a father in that we come from Him, He provides for us, and cares for us.

• When Jesus taught his disciples to pray: **This is how you should pray:“Our Father in heaven...”** (Matthew 6:9). In many languages, the word given for dad is often what babies naturally say early on because it's the easiest word to say. "Dada", "Tata", "Papa". In the Hebrew, the intimate word for Father is "Abba", and that's the word Jesus uses when he's teaching his disciples to pray. Prayer in one sense is like talking to our dad like a toddler talks to its dad.

• But prayer is not exactly like talking to dad, because God is "almighty"—he can do anything. In fact, he is the ***creator of heaven and earth***.

• You might know the famous opening verse of the Bible: **"In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth." (Genesis 1:1)**. Some people reject Christianity because they can't believe the story of how God created the world in Genesis. However, we must understand that the story in Genesis is not there to explain how God created the world. It's not a scientific account or a historical recount. Instead, it's a theological account. It is there to teach us: that the world was created,  who created it, and what place people have in the world as created beings.

#### Son

• I don't believe in God simply because I think the world needs a creator—though that is a factor. I primarily believe in God because of Jesus—who he was in history.

• ***I believe in Jesus Christ, his only son, our Lord*** — we call Jesus the "Son of God" because he was sent by God and he submits himself to the will of the Father. It's not because we believe the Son of God was created—we believe Jesus is eternal. It's not because we think Jesus is less than God—we believe He is fully God. It's because he was sent from the Father that we call him son, as the Bible does.

•  The word Christ is a Greek word with the same meaning as the Hebrew word Messiah: anointed one. In the Old Testament, and today, people anoint chosen rulers, i.e. kings. God had promised in the centuries leading up to Jesus through prophets that he would send an everlasting King. **"I will raise up your offspring after you… I will establish the throne of his kingdom forever." (2 Samuel 7:12–13)**. People in Jesus time were waiting for a forever-king, and the claim that Jesus is the "Christ" is a claim that he is that king that the people expected.

• It might therefore surprise you when you read that this supposed king ***was crucified, died and was buried*** — why would the king die like a criminal?

• The Bible says that Christ died for a reason, and it has to do with us. The apostle Paul writes to a church in the first century: **"Christ died for our sins according to the Scriptures." (1 Corinthians 15:3)**. Sin is what we call our rejection of God, and it is the greatest spiritual crime there is. If God created us, sustains us, and gives us a certain way to live and relate to him, it is our responsibility as His creatures to obey Him. If we reject Him, we cut ourselves off from life itself, and we deserve death.

• When Jesus died, he took our sin on Himself and bore the penalty that we deserve. Jesus says that whoever believes in Jesus' death can be cleansed of the guilt and enter into a restored relationship with God. Hundreds of years before Jesus a prophet named Isaiah described the death of the Messiah in this way, **"He was pierced for our transgressions ... by his wounds we are healed." (Isaiah 53:5)**. Christians interpret this verse as a prophecy foretelling Jesus' death and its spiritual implications — Jesus' death brings healing for all who trust in Him.

• How do we know Jesus died for our sin? Because ***On the third day he rose again from the dead*** — this is a miracle which demonstrates Jesus' trustworthiness and his kingship. He conquered sin and death for us.

• I believe Jesus' resurrection happened because it was conveyed by many eyewitnesses who had nothing to gain in speaking openly about his resurrection. The apostle Peter writes: **"We are witnesses of everything he did ... God raised him from the dead on the third day and caused him to be seen." (Acts 10:39–40)**. Peter died defending his faith, as did many other disciples. People die for all sorts of things, but nobody dies for a claim they know to be false. The disciples' boldness in spreading the news of Jesus' resurrection is one piece of evidence which bolsters our confidence that Jesus is risen.

• The spiritual implication of the resurrection is that Christians don't view death the same way anymore, because we believe there is something beyond death. The apostle Paul writes: **"We believe that Jesus died and rose again, and so we believe that God will bring with Jesus those who have fallen asleep in him." (1 Thessalonians 4:14)**.

• Jesus rose and ascended to heaven, and Christians believe that ***from there he will come to judge the living and the dead***. I don't know how or when Jesus will return, but I trust he will, because he has already demonstrated his reliability through his resurrection.

• God's judgement is good news, because justice is good. When someone commits a crime, we want there to be justice. The Bible says: **"God will bring every deed into judgment, including every hidden thing, whether it is good or evil." (Ecclesiastes 12:14)**.

• God's justice is good news until we realise that we ourselves are guilty of not obeying God and not loving others as we ought. But because Jesus died for sins, if we trust in him we can be forgiven. This means that when Jesus returns, those who trust in him will not be punished for their wrongdoing. Paul says: **"There is now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus." (Romans 8:1)**.

• Next week we will explore what Christians mean when we say ***I believe in the Holy Spirit***. For now, to summarise what we've seen, we might express the relationship between Father, Son, and Spirit as follows: the Father loves us; the Son lived among us; the Spirit lives in us.

• Read **the Apostles' Creed**

## The Holy Spirit

• Today we will focus on the final paragraph which begins with ***I believe in the Holy Spirit***.

• The word **"Holy"** means set apart for a special purpose. In this way, one could say that a toothbrush is holy. It is set apart for a purpose. I don't use my toothbrush to clean my toilet; I only use it on my teeth. In the Bible, the word is used to describe being set apart by God for a purpose. We are made new so that we can live life in a new way: for God, being the people God wants us to be.

• A **spirit** is that which animates us, guides us, gives our life its character and direction. Some people think that the job of the Holy Spirit is to give us visions and special powers. However, the job of the Holy Spirit is not to give us special powers; **the Holy Spirit helps us to be holy**.

• The Holy Spirit helps us to be holy by reminding us of who Jesus is, what he has done, and what he taught his disciples: **"The Holy Spirit ... will teach you all things and will remind you of everything I have said to you." (John 14:26)**.

## Made New

• How do you know if someone has the Holy Spirit? The Bible speaks of what the Spirit produces in our lives like fruit: **"the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness and self-control." (Galatians 5:22–23)**. As these things grow in our life, we are comforted that we do in fact have God's Spirit living in us.

• The Holy Spirit enters us into a new community (the church). That's why in this section of the creed we read about **"the holy catholic church"** . Here it is important to distinguish between what we mean by the catholic church and the Roman Catholic Church. The word catholic is an adjective which means universal or worldwide; this is what the creed is saying.

• Christians believe there are Christians scattered all over the world, and that to be a Christian is not based on ethnic or cultural background. The vision of the church we get in the Bible is a mixed group: **"I saw a great multitude that no one could count, from every nation, tribe, people and language." (Revelation 7:9)**.

• The Holy Spirit enters us into a new relationship with God—one grounded in **the forgiveness of sins**. Jesus died for us, the Holy Spirit helps us live as a forgiven people.

• We should explain what we mean by the word **sins**. None of us are perfect and we all rebel against the God who made us. Instead of living His way, we live our way. We don't treat God as we should, and we don't treat others as we should. The Anglican Prayer book says it like this: "We have left undone those things which we ought to have done, and we have done those things which we ought not to have done, and (spiritually) there is no health in us."

• We need forgiveness. Thankfully God provides forgiveness through Jesus: **"Everyone who believes in him receives forgiveness of sins through his name." (Acts 10:43)**.

• The Holy Spirit enters us into a new existence beyond the point of death because of **the resurrection of the body**. We believe it is by God's power that we are to experience life after death.

• The Apostle Paul puts it this way: **"If the Spirit who raised Jesus from the dead is living in you, he will also give life to your mortal bodies." (Romans 8:11, paraphrased)**.

• The Holy Spirit gives us new fulfillment. What this means is that rather than living life in an empty way, detached from the purpose for which God created us, by the Holy Spirit we are able to live life to the full in the here and now, by living life God's way. This is why we read about **the life everlasting** in this section of the creed.

• Jesus says: **"I have come that they may have life, and have it to the full." (John 10:10)**.
