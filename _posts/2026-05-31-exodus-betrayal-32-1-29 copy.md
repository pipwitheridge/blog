## Introduction: who or what is your god?
• Today: think about idols. More than think; confront. Who or what is your God?

• God is not just who we say God is; God is who/what we treat as God.
-----Tim Keller: "An idol is anything more important to you than God. Anything that
absorbs your heart and imagination more than God. Anything you seek to give you
what only God can give."-----

• Money?
-----Mike Tyson: "Money is my God. If you think God will help you then
quit your job and see how much He cares."-----

• Work?
-----Dale Carnegie (entrepreneur): "Throw yourself into some work you believe in with
all your heart, live for it, die for it, and you will find happiness that you had thought
could never be yours."-----

• Children?
-----"My children are my religion."-----

• Context: Exodus recap -- GOD HAS BEEN SO GOOD!
--> just received commands from God:
----- Ex 20:2-6: "I am the Lord your God, who brought you out of Egypt,
out of the land of slavery. You shall have no other gods before me. You shall not
make for yourself an image in the form of anything in heaven above or on the earth
beneath or in the waters below. You shall not bow down to them or worship
them..."-----

• Goal for today: see how Israel went wrong, learn. That's what the story is for.
-----1 Cor 10:6 (Paul on Israel's wilderness mistakes): "these things
occurred as examples to keep us from setting our hearts on evil things as they did.
Do not be idolaters, as some of them were..."-----

• Won't cover every detail — trying to get the big picture.

## 1. The human heart is an idol factory (vv. 1–6)
• Read Exodus 32:1–4.
----- "When the people saw that Moses was so long in coming down from the mountain,
they gathered around Aaron and said, “Come, make us gods who will go before us. As
for  this fellow Moses who brought us up out of Egypt, we don’t know what has
happened to him.” Aaron answered them, “Take off the gold earrings that your wives,
your sons and your daughters are wearing, and bring them to me.” 3 So all the people
took off  their earrings and brought them to Aaron. 4 He took what they handed him
and made it into an idol cast in the shape of a calf, fashioning it with a tool. Then they
said, “These are your gods, Israel, who brought you up out of Egypt.” -----

• Lesson 1: idolatry begins when waiting for God becomes intolerable; can be fast.
• Irony: how long has God waited for Israel!

• Lesson 2: idolatry happens when forgetting God becomes tolerable; national sin.
• v3 - All the people took off their earrings and brought them to Aaron.
• When was the last time they brought materials together for construction? Tabernacle.

• Lesson 3: anything can become an idol. Anything in the sky or the earth.
----- John Calvin: "the human heart is an idol factory." -----
• Why a gold calf? Seems strange.
-----Ps. 106.20, ‘They exchanged their glorious God for an image of a bull, which eats
grass."-----

• This is something we do! You might think we are Westerners – we don't have idols.
• There's no way we'd construct a gold calf to symbolise our God [show wallstreet bull]
• Ok... we did it one time! We're not like Eastern religions surely...
• Illustration: Indian pastor visits America.

• Some idolatry appears strange. Have you ever met someone passionate about
something that you cannot understand? Some people have strange things they
make number 1 — board games, plush toys, golf, knitting… one persons idol is
another persons oddity. All idols are foolish in God's sight.

Can you imagine what God must think when he sees any
of our idols—work, education. Don’t you know that you’re working to create a small
amount of change in a world, and that I created the world? Don’t you know all your
learning is truth that is only true because I made it so?

• Most idols are cultural norms: gold calf = status symbol? Us = house.
• Imagine: "they exchanged their glorious God for a house, and some grass"
• JC worst saying Matt 8:20:
-----“Foxes have dens and birds have nests, but the Son of
Man has no place to lay his head.”-----

• Hold on! How do we tell if it's worship? I want / have / like a house!
• Talking this week about running; by nature takes up time; is time the barometer?
• Is thought the barometer? That would make work difficult!

• Not just having something ... wanting something ... liking something...  but:
• Idolatry = treating a created thing as if it were your creator.

• Lesson 4: idolatry can be expressed in our words
• "These are your gods, Israel, who brought you up out of Egypt."
• What do you praise with your words during the week?
• What do you attribute your value as a person to?
• "My life would [lose all value if I did not have] ___" OR [be finally fulfilled if I had].

• Side-point: idolatry can be hidden behind our words.
• Note v5-6.
----- When Aaron saw this, he built an altar in front of the calf and announced,
“Tomorrow there will be a festival to the LORD (YHWH).” 6 So the next day the
people rose early and sacrificed burnt offerings and presented fellowship
offerings. Afterward they sat down to eat and drink and got up to
indulge in revelry."-----
• They baptised their idolatry in religious language and may have even thought
what they were doing was pleasing to YHWH.

• Morning church: be careful how you speak about good things to your children.
-----we might think we are helping our children by pressing the importance of
education, property, and financial stability, but let us evaluate our words to see if we
are simply passing down our idols to the next generation.-----

• Lesson 5: idolatry can be expressed in our actions.
• idolatry = bowing down to created things -- submission -- following.
• Not always words. Can be implicit: Matthew 19:16-22 Rich Young Ruler.

## 2. Idols promise life but lead to death (vv. 7-10)

• Read Exodus 32:7–10.
----- Then the Lord said to Moses, “Go down, because your people, whom you brought
up out of Egypt, have become corrupt. They have been quick to turn away from what
I commanded them and have made themselves an idol cast in the shape of a calf. They
have bowed down to it and sacrificed to it and have said, ‘These are your gods, Israel,
who brought you up out of Egypt.’ I have seen these people,” the Lord said to Moses,
“and they are a stiff-necked people. 10 Now leave me alone so that my anger may burn
against them and that I may destroy them. Then I will make you into a great
nation.” -----

• Israel bows down to a false God; yet God sees their true stature: stiff-necked.

• Note the fierce anger of God. Why is God angry?

• Idols in themself represented evil practices.
—> Canaanite idols (Baal and Asherah) involved fertility cults and ritual prostitution.
—> Worship of Molech involved child sacrifice.

• They forgot God who cared for them.

• Note how it's put in Rom 1:21-24:
----- "Although they knew God, they neither glorified him as God nor
gave thanks to him, but their thinking became futile and their foolish hearts were
darkened. 22 Although they claimed to be wise, they became fools 23 and exchanged
the glory of the immortal God for images made to look like a mortal human being and
birds and animals and reptiles. 24 Therefore God gave them over in the sinful
desires of their hearts..." -----

• Idolatry becomes its own punishment.

• Illustration: John Ruskin 1800s:
-----Lately in a wreck of a Californian ship, one of the passengers fastened a belt
about him with two hundred pounds of gold in it, with which he was found afterwards
at the bottom. Now, as he was sinking—did he have the gold? or did the gold
have him?-----

• Many people in life set themselves up for destruction by what they worship.

• Esau gave up birthright for bowl of stew. Hebrews calls him "godless" and warns
against sexual immorality.

• Children: worst story - Abraham offers up Isaac, yet we offer up our children
to a way of life (super-high-stress) that leads to their peril.

• Money --> work all your life and finally get that bank balance; but that
bank balance always had you wrapped around its finger. But ends in death.

• Relationship --> finally got the relationship but it cannot
survive because you've put your partner on a pedestal.

• The person who labours their whole life for their house; they eventually get the
house but they might not admit that the house always had them. It had their soul.

• Good gifts make terrible gods. They lead to destruction; corruption.

• That's the passive judgement; but there's also the active. "Anger burn against them."

• Why does God care beyond that? Why does he get angry?

• If we think God's judgement is an overreaction, we have underreacted to sin.

• I once heard a someone talking about God's judgement, and he was talking about
how Jesus describes those in hell as "weeping and gnashing". He said he would
not be a gnasher but a weeper, because he knows there's no reason he could
possibly be angry with God. Moses is the same — notice that in all the words he uses
to plead with God in Israel's defence, he never says they don't deserve judgement.

• We don't see a defence of Israel's actions; we don't see a defense of Aaron's
leadership or lack of leadership.

• But we still see God relent. There is hope.

## 3. There is hope for idol worshippers (vv. 11–35)

• Read Exodus 32:11–14.
----- 11 But Moses sought the favor of the Lord his God. “Lord,” he said, “why should
your anger burn against your people, whom you brought out of Egypt with great
power and a mighty hand? 12 Why should the Egyptians say, ‘It was with evil
intent that he brought them out, to kill them in the mountains and to wipe them
off the face of the earth’? Turn from your fierce anger; relent and do not bring
disaster on your people. 13 Remember your servants Abraham, Isaac and Israel,
to whom you swore by your own self: ‘I will make your descendants as numerous
as the stars in the sky and I will give your descendants all this land I promised them,
and it will be their inheritance forever.’” 14 Then the Lord relented and did not bring
on his people the disaster he had threatened.-----

• Why does God relent? For the sake of His people; His promises; the nations.

• How does God relent? Does He change his mind?
--> We don't know the inner workings of God but we see that God
does choose to listen to a mediator; his chosen mediator.

• Psalm 106:21–23 puts it like this:

----- 21 They forgot the God who saved them,
    who had done great things in Egypt,
22 miracles in the land of Ham
    and awesome deeds by the Red Sea.
23 So he said he would destroy them—
    had not Moses, his chosen one,
stood in the breach before him
    to keep his wrath from destroying them. -----

• Why does God listen to a mediator? Especially one like
Moses who himself is not
perfect - would go on to break all ten commands at once.

• God is merciful. It is in his nature to exercise justice and mercy. Like a good father.

• But there is tension in exericsing justice and mercy! Read Ex 34:6–7:
----- "The Lord, the Lord, the compassionate and gracious God, slow to anger,
abounding in love and faithfulness, maintaining love to thousands, and
forgiving wickedness, rebellion and sin. Yet he does not leave the
guilty unpunished."-----

• This is why Moses has a chance to mediate; to stand and say "do not destroy them,
God, father, forgive them, for they do not know what they do."

• Except Moses didn't say that part.

• Moses' work is not finished. Read Exodus 32:30:
-----"The next day Moses said to the people, “You have
committed a great sin. But now I will go up to the Lord; perhaps I can make
atonement for your sin.”-----

• You might be thinking, Moses mediated for them; who will mediate for me? Who will
stand in the breach?

• 1 Timothy 2:5
----- "For there is one God and one mediator between God and mankind,
the man Christ Jesus."-----

• What's needed is the atoning lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world.
• What's needed is the son of God to stand in the breach and plead our case.
• God gives us exactly what we need. 1 John 4:7:
----- "This is love, not that we loved God, but that He loved us, and gave His son as
an atoning sacrifice for our sins." -----

• Acts 17: a few years after Jesus' death and resurrection, Paul in Athens:
-----“People of Athens! I see that in every way you are very religious. I walked around,
looked carefully at your objects of worship, and I even found an altar with this
inscription: to an unknown god. So you are ignorant of the very thing you
worship...
——how I felt about a friend who poured himself into Bitcoin; I asked him
one day what it was and he said, "I don't really know..." ——Paul proclaims the God
who has made himself known——
...The God who made the world and everything in it is the Lord of heaven and earth
and does not live in temples built by human hands. 25 And he is not served by human
hands, as if he needed anything. Rather, he himself gives everyone life and breath
and everything else. 26 From one man he made all the nations, that they should
inhabit the whole earth; and he marked out their appointed times in history
and the boundaries of their lands. 27 God did this so that they would seek him
and perhaps reach out for him and find him, though he is not far from any one of us.
‘For in him we live and move and have our being.’ As some of your own
poets have said, ‘We are his offspring.’
“Therefore since we are God’s offspring, we should not think that the divine being
is like gold or silver or stone—an image made by human design and skill.
In the past God overlooked such ignorance, but now he commands all people
everywhere to repent. For he has set a day when he will judge the world with
justice by the man he has appointed. He has given proof of this to everyone by raising
him from the dead.”-----

## 4. Flee from idols

• We still need to take idols seriously even if you've been a Christian a while.

• 1 Cor 10:11-14:
-----These things happened to them as examples and were written down as
warnings for us, on whom the culmination of the ages has come. 12 So, if you think
you are standing firm, be careful that you don’t fall! No temptation has overtaken
you except what is common to mankind. And God is faithful; he will not let you
be tempted beyond what you can bear. But when you are tempted, he will also
provide a way out so that you can endure it. Therefore, my dear friends,
flee from idolatry.-----

• Note: not walk, not jog – flee! Fleeing takes effort! And it can cost!

• Joseph fled sexual sin, lost cloak (bad time w cloaks), reputation, position, career,
freedom, years of his life.

• He lost so much but what he was able to keep made it all worth it: God.

• Flee from idols by running to God.

• Follow the example of Jesus.

• Jesus was not lazy: we don't know what Israel were doing 40 days before
tempation; but we know what Jesus was doing 40 days before his. Alert; fasting.

• When tempted, remembered God and His word.

• Matt 4:8–10:
----- Again, the devil took him to a very high mountain and showed him all the
kingdoms of the world and their splendor. “All this I will give you,” he said,
“if you will bow down and worship me.”
Jesus said to him, “Away from me, Satan! For it is written:
‘Worship the Lord your God, and serve him only.’” -----

• Some here may have physical idols. Get rid OR don't worship!

• Some of you have thought of idols along the way. Time to put idols away.

• Don't put your emotions and your passions away. The human heart is a worshipping
heart. Worship God with all your heart!
— He created you.
— He loves you.
— You need Him for life.

• Finish with prayer of repentance:

*Heavenly Father, Thank you for creating me and sustaining me.
I am sorry for worshipping created things.
Thank you for your patience and mercy towards me.
Thank you that Jesus resisted temptation to worship idols.
Thank you that Jesus died in my place for my sin.
Thank you that Jesus’ resurrection brings me life.
Please help me, by your Spirit, to follow Jesus always.
Please help me, by your Spirit, to worship You alone.
Amen.*
