# What should be our target in life? (10 minutes)

• Story --> The world's best archer... or so he claimed. Film himself shooting an arrow a great distance, then film the end scene, the arrow planted in the bullseye of a target. People came and watched... What he would do is he would shoot the arrow wherever he wanted, then draw a target around where the arrow fell, and would say "see, bullseye every time!"

• We do something similar with our lives. Often, we simply live how we want, let it take its course, and decide along the way whether we've been successful, often skewed by where our life has gone. But what if we are ignoring life's true target?

---SLIDE---

• What should be our target in life? Money. Romance. Career. Marriage/kids. Doing good; staying comfortable.

• Want to paint a picture of the Christian answer to that; it comes from 1st book of Bible.

---SLIDE---

• Story --> Genesis. A story which illustrates why we're are here and what has gone wrong.

• Chapter 1 = God creates the universe; light, matter, biology, consciousness.

• Chapter 2 = first people Adam and Eve = Hebrew means "man" and "source of life". Made to know and love God.

• Everything is good; represented as a garden paradise.

• Chapter 3 = everything goes wrong. All sorts of problems enter the world. But before we get there...

---SLIDE---

## Question: Why do you think humans keep creating problems for ourselves and for each other? (7 min group discussion)

• In the garden paradise for Adam and Eve – there was one rule: don't eat from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil.

• Chapter 3:1–16 – just listen. Keep in mind: story to highlight bigger spiritual truths. Snake tries to turn everything upside down. Tempts them to eat from the one tree God had told them not to eat from.

• What stands out to you? 2 things stand out for me:

---SLIDE---

• *Did God really say...? (Gen 3:1).*

---SLIDE---

• *You will be like God. (Gen 3:5).*

• We were made to love God and trust him, but humanity decided that we should be the ones who determine right and wrong. We put ourselves in God's place.

---SLIDE---

• The Bible calls that sin. Definition: Missing the mark of what life is meant to be by putting ourselves in God’s place instead of living under his rule.

• From this, all sorts of problems enter the world.

• Shame between humans and God; blame between husband and wife; murder between brothers.... the social ripples spread everywhere.

• God is angry with sin; the ultimate judgement for sin is death and seperation from God.

• Sin is universal; its deeply engrained; it leads to seperation from God eternally; it is not a problem we can solve ourselves.

---SLIDE---

# Jesus' target (10 minutes)

• I don't know what you know about Jesus, but he is at the centre of Christianity.

• Jesus' addressed lots of problems in his day: sickness, power abuse, natural disaster, social alienation.

• Jesus' target was to address the root problem of humanity: sin.
*Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners. (1 Tim 1:15).*

• Recap and preview. Next week: did Jesus hit his target to save sinners? If so, how?
