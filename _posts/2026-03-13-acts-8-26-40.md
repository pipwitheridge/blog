## How can a total outsider come to God?

Have you ever felt like a total outsider to God?

There are two big reasons people often feel like outsiders to God: they don't want God, they feel like God doesn't want them. Maybe you've experienced one of these.

Do you ever wonder whether God cares about you? How do you know that he does?

In our passage we see someone who probably felt like an outsider to God.

Passage ---

There are two things that would make evangelism difficult here: first, here we see this man is an outsider: racially, geographically, relationally/sexually, professionally. But the gospel is unstoppable—and it won't let these boundary markers stop it. Second, Phillip evangelism in this moment would be really awkward. The man is riding in his carriage! But Phillip won't let the fact that the man is on his carriage stop him either.

Passage ---

Phillip meets him where he is at. That's what evangelism is all about: meeting people where they are at. You have to go to them.

The best evangelism begins by asking questions: what are you reading? What are you thinking about?

Passage ---

What's the passage he's wrestling with? It's all about how Jesus was willing to become like an outsider so that anyone—including you—can be close to God. Jesus endured injustice so that we could get grace.

The man feels so welcome that he is keen to do something which shows he's in.

Passage ---

What is baptism? It's an outward sign of something that happens inwardly. We are united to Jesus in his death and resurrection, and we experience new life with God. It's a symbol of God's power to save—even the outsider!
